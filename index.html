About Me

My name is Ángel. I was born in Madrid, Spain, and moved to New Jersey when I was three years old. I come from a huge family, I have 7 siblings and 16 neices and nephews. That is as of now, but that number grows almost every year!I speak Spanish, English, and French. If you count programming languages, then I am also pretty good at a few of those as well. I am currently a junior at Stevens Institute of Technology studying Computer Engineering, and as a senior I will concentrate my major in Networks & Security. I currently work on campus as a research assistant and, in the summer of 2023, I will be an intern at ADP doing application development within the Global Product and Technology group.

I chose to study engineering after taking a class called "real world engineering" my freshman year of high school to get my STEM elective out of the way early. This class piqued my interest of engineering by teaching me AutoCAD, 3d printing, and some simple circuits. We combined these topics into 3 big projects being a tower which had to hold 30 pounds of weight, a maglev styrofoam racecar, and CO2 airplanes. I knew going into high school that I did not want to study business, since that was what my father and older brother did. After taking this class, I knew I wanted to be an engineer.

My first Engineering Design project at Stevens was to build and deploy an Arduino weather station. My team and I programmed the board to gather data on temperature, humidity, and light intensity. Pictured here is the board with the sensors attached.

In my first semester at Stevens, I also made a Nintendo Switch in SOLIDWORKS. My job was the dock, and my team members made the screen and the joycons. Pictured here is my final design for the dock. For the full assembly, click the picture!

Self-Navigating Robot

My second semester at Stevens, I assembled and programmed a robot in Arduino to navigate a track to certain points and evade obstacles in it's path. The robot's chassis was also designed in SOLIDWORKS and 3d printed. Pictured here is my team's robot.

Truss Modeler

My third semester at Stevens, I had my first Engineering Design project not related to CPE. I had to design a truss that would withstand a given load, meet size and material specifications, and be under the target price. Pictured here is my truss design, click to see how it performed.

Amplifier + Crossover Filter Network

My fourth semester at Stevens, I had to design a high and low pass filter in the same circuit and pass a signal through it. The circuit was first modeled in MATLAB using Simulink, and then built physically. It had two amplifiers, a high and a low pass filter, and two buffers before connecting to the physical amplifier which would play the filtered signal. The whole circuit didn't fit in this picture, but click to see more of them!
